Project information

  • Project: Fund-proposal plan for Roche Pharmaceuticals on its DVTs project -Aim to prevent blood clots post-knee/hip replacement surgery, a significant concern given that up to 40% of post-surgical patients are at risk of developing DVTs.
  • Business goal : By facilitating early detection and remote recovery, critical hospital resources can be conserved. This is crucial considering that in 2020, the average cost per hospital stay in Canada was approximately $7,200.
  • Technology: Microsoft Project, Microsoft office suite, PowerBI.

Role and Contributions

Role: Project coordinator.

  • Developed a fund-proposal plan to request a government funding of CAD 60,000 for the early detection and remote recovery of DVTs in elderly Canadian population.
  • Used business analysis, cost estimation, resource estimation, project scheduling skills to develop a project plan for the development and launching of the application upon being granted with the requested budget within 8 months timeframe.
  • Researched and developed a platform for seamless remote doctor-patient communication for post-surgical patients, enhancing patient care and satisfaction, and decreasing the average length of hospital stays, resulting in resource optimization and a 15% cost reduction in hospital resource allocation.
  • Developed comprehensive reports, presentations, and extensive project documentation, including user manuals, technical specifications, and implementation guides, providing detailed insights into project progress, cost-saving measures, and performance metrics, facilitating informed decision-making, enhancing user adoption, minimizing training time, improving milestone tracking, ensuring project timelines, and contributing to the ongoing success and scalability of the project.
